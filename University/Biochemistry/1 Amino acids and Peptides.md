Almost all common amino acids in proteins occur in the L-configuration. Less than 1 % are found in the D-configuration, often serving a precise structural purpose and is introduced by enzyme-catalyzed reactions after protein synthesis.
Asymmetric active sites of enzymes make it possible for cells to synthesize exclusively L-isomers.

#### Nonpolar, aliphatic R groups
- Glycine (G, Gly)
- Alanine (A, Ala)
- Proline (P, Pro)
- Valine (V, Val)
- Leucine (L, Leu)
- Isoleucine (I, Ile)
- Methionine (M, Met)
#### Polar, uncharged R groups
- Serine (S, Ser)
- Threonine (T, Thr)
- Cysteine (C, Cys)
- Asparagine (N, Asn)
- Glutamine (Q, Gln)
#### Aromatic R groups
- Phenylalanine (F, Phe)
- Tyrosine (Y, Tyr)
- Tryptophan (W, Trp)
#### Positively charged R groups
- Lysine (K, Lys)
- Arginine (R, Arg)
- Histidine (H, His)
#### Negatively charged R groups
- Aspartate/Aspartic acid (D, Asp)
- Glutamate/Glutamic acid (E, Glu)
#### Uncommon
- Selenocysteine
- Pyrrolysine (occurs in methanogenic bacteria)
