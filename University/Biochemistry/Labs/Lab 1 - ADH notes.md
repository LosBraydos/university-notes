Monitor catalytic enzyme activity via UV/VIS spectrophotometry

YADH1 reduces acetaldehyde with NADH into ethanol and NAD+.
In this lab, the reverse reaction will be performed. NAD+ and ethanol is provided oxidizing ethanol into acetaldehyde and reduced NAD+ into NADH.

Kinetic rate will be monitored.
![[Pasted image 20260329164546.png]]


The objectives of this experiment are to:
- Determine the initial reaction rate of an enzyme catalyzed reaction
- Determine the kinetic parameters 𝐯(𝐦𝐚𝐱) , 𝐊(𝐌) ,and 𝐤(𝐜𝐚𝐭) for YADH1
- Discuss the meaning of 𝐯(𝐦𝐚𝐱) , 𝐊(𝐌) , 𝐤(𝐜𝐚𝐭) and the ratio 𝐤(𝐜𝐚𝐭) /𝐊(𝐌)
- Discuss how and why different experimental factors affect the rate of an enzyme-catalyzed reaction.

NADH absorbs light at wavelength = 340 nm. NAD+ does not. Change in absorbance at 340 nm is directly proportional to concentration of NADH and ethanol (oxidation of 1 mol alcohol is equivalent to reduction of 1 mol NAD+)

## Materials and equipment required
Solutions: (These will be provided to you) 
- Phosphate buffer: 100 mM, pH 7.15
- NAD+: 100 mM, pH 7.0
- Yeast alcohol dehydrogenase 1 (YADH1): 0.33 mg/ml in phosphate buffer, MW = 141 kDa (kg/mol). Always store the enzyme on ice!
- Ethanol: 200 mM diluted in phosphate buffer

Note: Phosphate buffer (pH 7.15) is used to prevent the pH of the reaction from lowering due to the formation of protons during the oxidation of alcohol.

Equipment:
- UV/Visible spectrophotometer at a constant wavelength, λ = 340 nm
- Micropipettes
- Plastic cuvettes (optical path length l =1 cm). Make sure they are clean!

Activity will be measured at seven different substrate (ethanol) concentrations:
- 2 mM
- 5 mM
- 10 mM
- 20 mM
- 40 mM
- 60 mM
- 100 mM

Additionally a blank measurement is taken for each concentration(?) and an auto-reaction measurement at the highest substrate concentration with NAD+ and *without* enzyme
