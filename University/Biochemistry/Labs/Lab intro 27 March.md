Lab introductions
- Enzyme kinetics (ADH)
	- Enzymes - biological catalysts
	- Increase the reaction rate by lowering the activation energy
	- Not consumed in chemical reactions, and do not alter the equilibrium of a reaction (catalyse reverse reaction as well!)
	- Alcohol DeHydrogenase (ADH)
		- Catalyze the oxidation of primary and secondary alcohols to aldehydes and ketones, both forward and reverse
		- Oxidizes substrate by transferring a hydrogen to an electron acceptor, commonly NAD+ or FAD
		- Reaction will be monitored using UV-VIS spectrophotometry.
- Protein purification (eYFP)
	- enhanced Yellow Fluorescence Protein
	- Derived from GFP
	- 239 amino acids (ca 27 kDa)
	- Fluoresces because of the chromophore formed by a tripeptide: serine-tyrosine-glycine
	- Recombinant protein expression.
		- Purification by His-tag. Histidine likes to stick to divalent metal ions (Ni2+, Mg2+...). A sequence of histidine on a protein of interest can be used to separate from a mixture by introducing metal ions.
